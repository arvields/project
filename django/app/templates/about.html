ABOUT

General Objectives of the System



The primary objective of this classification system is to employ a knowledge base, decision tables, and a decision tree algorithm for the morphological classification of Indo-Pacific coastal fish ichthyoplankton. This system aims to automate the classification method for ichthyoplankton, grouping them into their respective categories, genus, and families based on meristic measurements and sample characteristics.

Utilizing a decision tree algorithm within a questionnaire connected to the knowledge base, the system will initially provide a classification result and then generate the final classification in the scorecard using decision tables. Our proposed solution seeks to suggest or generate fish groups, families, and genus aligned with specified parameters, offering a practical and efficient tool for marine biologists, fisheries scientists, and other stakeholders involved in ichthyoplankton classification.

This system's objective is to advance automated classification methods, simplifying the classification of ichthyoplankton groups, families, and genus.




























Specific Objectives of the System

The specific objective of this classification system that incorporates a knowledge base, decision tables, and a decision tree algorithm is to efficiently classify ichthyoplankton into their respective groups, families, and genus based on morphological features and meristic characters/measurements. This system is designed to eliminate the need for machine learning expertise, leveraging decision tables and a decision tree algorithm for effective classification.

At the heart of this endeavor is an intelligent questionnaire, seamlessly integrating data entry, calculation, and classification into a unified and streamlined process. This novel approach aims to improve efficiency, reduce redundancy, and automate the classification process, ultimately delivering a quicker and more effective outcome for users.

The knowledge base functions as a digital repository, housing data on the morphometrics and meristic characteristics of the ichthyoplankton fish group, family, and genusâ€”resembling an electronic version of the reference book by Jeffrey M. Leis and Brooke M. Carson-Ewart used by the study group.

The decision tree algorithm analyzes recorded data and conducts the classification process within the questionnaire and knowledge base, transforming the questionnaire into an intelligent tool. The resulting outputs include displaying the shape characteristic and group number to which the specimen belongs, along with presenting potential candidate families as the initial step in the classification.

To score the morphometric and meristic values, particularly in determining candidate fish families and narrowing down options, a decision table is employed. The decision table provides a structured and clear representation of rules, facilitating a transparent interpretation of the classification process leading to the final result.
